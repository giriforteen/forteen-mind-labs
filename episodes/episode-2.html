The Psychology:

Neural pathways are like trails. The more you walk them, the deeper they get. Your brain formed relationship pathways in childhood. When you meet someone new, your brain scans: Do they trigger the familiar feeling? If yes: Pattern recognized. This is safe.

Safe doesn't mean healthy. It means known. You're not choosing badly. You're choosing automatically.

The Psychology:

That friendship isn't about the friend. It's about the parent who was inconsistent. The sibling who only noticed you when they wanted something. Your brain learned: Love is earned. Equal give and take. You feel valued.

Pro Tip #4 If all your friendships have the same plot, you're not unlucky with friends. You're following a script written in childhood.

Why You Keep Buying Tickets

If you know the movie is terrible, why keep watching?

You're watching for redemption.

If this person finally chooses you, it means you were always worthy. If this boss sees your value, it means you always had it. If this friend shows up, it means you were always worth showing up for.

You're not repeating the pattern because you're broken. You're trying to fix something that broke long ago.
